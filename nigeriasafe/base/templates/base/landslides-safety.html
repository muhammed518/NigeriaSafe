Landslide Safety

How to prepare for and react to a landslide.

Before a Landslide

Landslides can occur quickly and with little warning. Being observant is your best defense.

  • Know the Warning Signs:
    • Doors or windows sticking or jamming for the first time.
    • New cracks appearing in plaster, tile, brick, or foundations.
    • Outside walls, walks, or stairs beginning to pull away from the building.
    • Slowly developing, widening cracks on the ground or on paved areas.
    • Bulging ground at the base of a slope.
    • Water breaking through the ground surface in new locations.
    • Fences, retaining walls, utility poles, or trees tilting or moving.
  • Listen to Nature: A faint rumbling sound that increases in volume is noticeable as the landslide nears. The ground may begin shifting under your feet.
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Have a "go-bag" ready with documents, water, and essentials in case you need to evacuate quickly.

During a Landslide

Your reaction time is critical.

  • Stay Alert: Many landslide fatalities occur when people are sleeping. Listen to local news stations on a battery-powered radio for warnings of heavy rainfall.
  • Evacuate Immediately: If you suspect imminent danger, leave immediately. Inform neighbors if you can.
  • If You Are Indoors: Move to a second story if possible. Stay out of the path of the landslide.
  • If You Are Outdoors: Run across the slope (perpendicular to the flow), not down it. If escape is not possible, curl into a tight ball and protect your head.
  • Driving: Watch the road for collapsed pavement, mud, fallen rocks, and other indications of possible debris flows.

After a Landslide

The danger may not be over even after the slide stops.

  • Stay Away: Flooding or additional slides may occur after a landslide. Do not enter the slide area.
  • Check for Injured Persons: Look for injured and trapped persons near the slide, without entering the direct slide area. Direct professional rescuers to their locations.
  • Report Hazards: Look for and report broken utility lines and damaged roadways and railways to appropriate authorities.
  • Check Foundations: Check the building foundation, chimney, and surrounding land for damage.
  • Replanting: Replant damaged ground as soon as possible since erosion caused by loss of ground cover can lead to flash flooding and additional landslides.

Water Safety

Essential tips for staying safe in and around water.

Before You Get in the Water

Learn to swim. Check weather and water conditions before going in. Always swim in designated areas supervised by lifeguards. Wear a life jacket when boating, regardless of swimming ability. Never swim alone; always bring a buddy.

While in the Water

Enter water feet first. Do not dive into unknown bodies of water. Watch out for currents, waves, and underwater hazards. Avoid alcohol before or during swimming or boating. Supervise children closely and never leave them unattended near water.

Recognizing and Responding to Distress

Know the signs of drowning, which can be silent. If someone is in trouble, call for help. Use the "Reach, Throw, Don't Go" method: try to reach the person with an object, throw them a flotation device, but do not enter the water to rescue them unless you are trained.
